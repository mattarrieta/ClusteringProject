HEREBY GRANTS A
Common Victualer's License
To:  DOWNTOWN CREAMERY
Licensee: DOWNTOWN CREAMERY LLC - WALTER ENCALADA
At:  410 WAVERLY ST 6/*5  FRAMINGHAM, MA in said City.
Hours: SUNDAY THROUGH SATURDAY 5:30 A.M. TO 10:00 P.M.
Until FRIDAY, DECEMBER 31, 2021
and at that place only.
This license is granted and accepted upon the express condition that the person or persons above licensed, shall, in all respects, fulfill, and conform
to all requirements of the laws relating to licensing of Common Victualers; and it may be suspended or revoked for any violation of such.
Patrons are not permitted to bring alcoholic beverages on the premises for their own consumption. Non Alcoholic Common Victualer
licenses are not permitted to keep alcoholic beverages on the the premises except for a reasonable small quantity that is used in preparation
of certain specialty foods
THIS LICENSE IS NOT TRANSFERABLE UNTIL APPROVED BY THE
FRAMINGHAM BOARD OF LICENSE COMMISSIONERS
EXTRACTS FROM THE GENERAL LAWS, CHAPTER 140.
SECTION 8. A common victualer who, upon request, on any day but Sunday, refuses to supply food to a stranger or traveler shall be punished by
a fine of not more than fifty dollars.
SECTION 9. If, in the opinion of the licensing authorities, a licensee as an innholder or a common victualer ceases to be engaged in the business
he is licensed to pursue, or fails to maintain upon his premises the implements and facilities required by this chapter, they shall immediatedely
revoke his license. If a licensee at any time conducts his licensed business in an improper manner, the licensing authorities, after notice to the
licensee and reasonable opportunity for a hearing, may upon satisfactory proof thereof suspend or revoke his license. A common victualer who
violates section eight shall forfeit his license. A licensee who is convicted a second time of the violation of any of the provisions of sections six to
eighteen, inclusive, shall forfeit his license.
SECTION 20. Whoever assumes to be an innholder or common victualer without being licensed as such under this chapter shall forfeit one
hundred dollars.
SECTION 21. Whoever is convicted a third time of a violation of any provision of the preceding sections, except those contained in sections
seven and eight, shall, in addition to the penalties before provided, be punished by imprisonment for not more than three months.

KEN’S STEAK HOUSE, INC., DBA KEN’S STEAK HOUSE, 95 WORCESTER RD. 
AMENDMENT TO APPROVED TEMPORTARY OUTDOOR SEATING
ONE-DAY ENTERTAINMENT LICENSE APPLICATION
The Board reviewed the following information: (1) Letter from Attorney Eggers; (2) Corporate Vote; (3) 
Site Plan; (4) Sample Mobile Bar; (5) Tent Details and Related Correspondence with Health Inspector
Mendez; (6) 2020 Approved Plan; (7) Entertainment License Application. 
Attorney Chris Eggers introduced himself on behalf of Ken’s Steak House. He described the new seating
plan, which involves 8 smaller tents, a tent that will cover an entertainment area, and a mobile bar. He
said the bar will be a service bar for servers to bring drinks to customers seated at their tables. 
Attorney Eggers said the requested entertainment license is for outdoor entertainment. He said there
will be more than 25 feet between performers and guests. Performances will include one and three
performers at a time with an instrument or pre-recorded music with vocals. 
Ms. Porter noted that the previously approved outdoor dining hours were until 10 p.m. but the
entertainment application includes hours until 11 p.m. Attorney Eggers said he would like the outdoor
dining hours to match the entertainment hours since the COVID-related curfew has been rescinded. 
Mr. Barnosky made a motion to approve the temporary restaurant outdoor hours on Sunday from 12 to
11 p.m., Monday through Saturday 11:30 a.m. to 11 p.m., and the entertainment Thursday through
Saturday 6 p.m. to 11 p.m. until 60 days after the end of the Governor’s state of emergency, pending
approval from the treasurer and collector, police department traffic and safety, and subject to the
conditional approvals of the police department, fire department, health department, and inspectional
services read into the record during the public hearing. 
Ms. Porter seconded the motion. 
All voted in favor, 5-0. 
Medrano: Aye

Mr. Pologe said he and Ms. Wadland have concerns about a new request for a one-day license for
Exhibit A Brewery due to the number of days that will be included in the license. He said the Board
previously discussed requiring the business to obtain a permanent license. 
Ms. Wadland said that each one-day license covers 30 days and there are multiple applications under

Kelsey Roth introduced himself to the Board and explained that the Building Department wanted the
parking lot where the brewery is located zoned for restaurant use, which got approved. He said the
business cannot apply for an annual on-premises license because the owner currently holds a
wholesaler liquor license and cannot legally hold both. He said in order for the business to obtain an
annual license it would have to be a farmer/brewer license, which would require them to build an actual
brewery on the licensed premises. He also said they cannot apply for one-day licenses while an
application for an annual license is in process, so the one-day licenses are the only means of operating
currently. He said building a brewery on-site is the long-term plan, though he is unable to provide a
timeline. 
Commissioners Wadland and Pologe said they just wanted to ensure there were no concerns of the
Board before approving the one-day licenses. No members voiced concern.
