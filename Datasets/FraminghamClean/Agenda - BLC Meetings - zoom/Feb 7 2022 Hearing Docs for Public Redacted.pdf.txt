Discuss Board’s Ability to Issue Fines for Alcohol Violations
Presentation by City Solicitor, Chris Petrini, Amanda Zuretti
They reviewed the proposed amended Alcohol Rules and regulations for the City. One of the questions was the
ability of the Board to imposed monetary penalty upon licensees
The statues allow local licenses authorities to impose suspensions and revocation of licenses but there no
language allowing monetary penalties by the ABCC regulations. This provision existed in the 6th version of the
City regulations but it was deleted in the 7th version although there was language leftover, the phrase “ability to
impose monetary penalties” It should not have been there and created confusion. Mr. Pologe asked for
clarification on the powers of board on imposing fines or fees. 
The statutes of the ABCC allows the ABCC to impose fines but not the local authorities. 
Regarding administrative fees, there is a provision that allows the local board to impose reasonable service fees
that licensees must pay, including inspection fees. There is also a fee related to an investigation by the police dept. 
Mr. Petrini explained that it could be a sliding fee scale depending of the time spent by the police dept on the
investigation by using a formula, around $50-$60 per hour rate, an amount not to exceed, maybe a $1000.00. We
cannot charge for dispatching cruises to a location. Fees go to general fund not to the Police Dept. Mr. Petrini will
talk to Lt. McCarthy and come up some numbers.
