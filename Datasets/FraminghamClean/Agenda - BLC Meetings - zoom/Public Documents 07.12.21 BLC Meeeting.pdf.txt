TEMPORARY RESTAURANT OUTDOOR USE FOR PHASE II OPENING – THE BUCKLEY KITCHEN AND BAR, 
440 FRANKLIN ST. 
The Board reviewed the following information: (1) Application; (2) Site Plan. 
Zacharias Sairkos introduced himself to the Board and said the establishment is seeking to add
additional outdoor seating to the plan that the Board previously approved at an earlier meeting. 
Mr. Sairkos explained his alcohol and common victualer licenses are approved for 15 outdoor seats on
the patio, but they are hoping to temporarily add existing seats to the patio during the warmer months. 
The total business seating capacity will not change. The outdoor patio would have 24 seats. The patio
hours begin at 11 a.m. and end at 10 p.m., he said. 
Mr. Barnosky made a motion to approve new temporary restaurant outdoor use for phase II opening
from 11 a.m. to 10 p.m. and to add additional seats to existing patio according to the application –
subject to comments and conditions of the fire department, police department, licensing pending
certificate of inspection – total seats indoor and outdoor cannot exceed 60. 
Ms. Porter seconded the motion. 
Mr. Barnosky amended the motion to include patio hours Sunday through Thursday 11 a.m. to 10 p.m.;
Friday and Saturday 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. to be consistent with the previously approved patio hours. 
All voted in favor, 4-0. 
Wadland: Aye
Porter: Aye
Pologe: Aye
Barnosky: Aye

PUBLIC HEARING – CONSIDER AMENDING “TOWN OF FRAMINGHAM RULES AND REGULATIONS
GOVERNING ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES” CONTINUED FROM MARCH 9, 2020; NOVEMBER 4, OCTOBER 7, 
SEPTEMBER 6, AUGUST 19, JULY 22, 2019; JUNE 18, MAY 21, AND APRIL 30, 2018
The Board reviewed the following information: (1) 7th Proposed Revision June 2021 Redlined Draft; (2) 7th 
Proposed Revision June 2021 Clean Draft. 
The Board reviewed draft changes that have been incorporated into the Rules and Regulations over the
past several years. 
The Board discussed substantive changes to the Regulations, including removing language regarding
license transfers for financial compensation, removing the requirement for non-managerial staff of
licensed establishments to obtain Framingham server identifications, and removing limitations on the
size of glassware for alcoholic beverages. 
Ms. Wadland said the current Regulations prohibit BYOB, and asked if the Board wanted to remove the
prohibition and “be silent” on BYOB to give restaurants the opportunity to choose if they want to allow
patrons to bring their own alcohol. 
District 4 City Councilor Michael Cannon provided input on the size of glassware that is limited in the
current Rules and Regulations. 
Kelsey Roth from Exhibit A asked about the possibility of continuing the acceptance of online alcohol
training. He said attending in-person training can be a hardship for some employees. 
Mr. Barnosky said he would incorporate some of the changes discussed in the clean copy of the
proposed changes for the Board to review at its next meeting.
