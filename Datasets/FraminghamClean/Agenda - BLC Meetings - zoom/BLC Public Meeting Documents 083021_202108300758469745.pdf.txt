PUBLIC HEARING
TRANSFER OF ALL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES RETAIL LICENSE – HARINOM CORPORATION (DBA LIQUOR
WORLD), 235 OLD CONNECTICUT PATH
The Board reviewed the following information: (1) Draft Licensing Authority Certification; (2) Draft
License; (3) Letter from Attorney Matthew S. Porter Dated June 18, 2021; (4) Alcohol License
Applications; (5) Corporate Vote; (6) CORI Request Forms; (7) Proof of Citizenship; (8) Alcohol Server
Training Certificate; (9) Statement of Change of Supplemental Information; (10) Articles of Organization; 
(11) Assignment and Assumption of Lease; (12) Asset Purchase and Sale Agreement; (13) Letter of Intent; 
(14) Letter from Matthew S. Porter, Esq. with Promissory Note and Pledge; (15) Assignment and Pledge
of Liquor License and Alcohol Inventory; (16) DOR Certificate of Good Standing; (17) DUA Certificate of
Compliance; (18) Assistant Manager Application; (19) Monetary Transmittal Form; (20) Legal Notice; (21) 
Memo from Lt. Ruiz. 
Attorney Ian Hedges introduced himself to the Board, along with proposed manager Amita Pachani. 
Mr. Hedges said all four shareholders have experience operating other liquor stores in Massachusetts. 
Ms. Pachani said there will be no changes to the inventory or business operations, aside from a two-
hour reduction in hours on Sundays. She said there will be an ID scanner system for ensuring patrons are
21 or older.

Ms. Wadland noted that the application indicated that the applicant’s lease does not include payments
to the landlord based on alcohol sales, but that the lease does indicate the lessee would pay 2% of gross
sales. 
Mr. Hedges said he would verify the lease has not been amended and would amend the ABCC
application if necessary. 
Ms. Porter noted this is a same location transfer and comes with the recommendation of the Police
Department. 
Ms. Porter made a motion to approve the transfer of all alcoholic beverages retail license and pledge of
license and inventory from Fifth Avenue Liquors, Inc. to Harinom Corporation, DBA Liquor World, 235
Old Connecticut Path, with the hours of Monday to Saturday 8 a.m. to 11 p.m. and Sunday 10 a.m. to 9
p.m., and proposed manager Amita Pachani and assistant manager Jignesh Patel. 
Ms. Wadland seconded the motion. 
All voted in favor, 3-0. 
Pologe: Aye
Porter: Aye
Wadland: Aye

COVID ACCOMODATIONS
EXPIRATION DATE FOR RESTAURANT EXPANDED OUTDOOR TABLE SERVICE TO MIRROR CH. 20 OF THE
ACTS OF 2021
EXTEND ACCEPTANCE OF ONLINE ALCOHOL SERVER TRAINING CERTIFICATION
The Board reviewed the following information: (1) Press Release Dated June 28, 2021. 
Ms. Wadland said the Board can vote to mirror state regulations by allowing outdoor seating
applications until April 1, 2022. She said her interpretation is that the applications could be for seating
throughout Framingham’s normal outdoor seating period ending in October. 
Ms. Wadland made a motion to extend the expiration date for restaurant expanded outdoor table
service to mirror Ch. 20 of the Acts of 2021 and extend the acceptance of online alcohol server training
certification until September 1, 2021 when the new Rules and Regulations come into effect. 
Mr. Pologe seconded. 
Ms. Porter made an amendment to mirror the state regulations allowing applications through April 1, 
2022 for outdoor seating through the 2022 calendar year. 
Mr. Pologe seconded. 
All voted in favor, 3-0. 
Pologe: Aye
Porter: Aye
