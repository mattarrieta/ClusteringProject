Arany galuska
Arany Galushka (or Aranygaluska)  is a  traditional
 Hungarian dessert consisting of balls of yeast dough (galuska).
The balls are rolled in melted butter, and then rolled in a
mixture of sugar and crushed nuts (traditionally, walnuts),
assembled into layers, before being baked till golden. Arany
means gold or golden; galuska refers to the dumpling nature of
the balls of dough. Aranygaluska may be served with vanilla
custard. 
Arany Galushka
 Nancy Reagan popularized this dish in the [URL: "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States"] United States when
she served it at the [URL: "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_House"] White House for Christmas.[1]

Somloi Galushka
 Cookbook: Arany Galushka
Related desserts
Somloi Galuska bears similarity to an English trifle. Its galuska (dumplings) are made of sponge cake,
which are layered with vanilla custard, chocolate sauce, raisins, and rum. It may be garnished with whipped
cream.[2]
